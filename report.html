Introduction

What is the essential story being told by your site and what type of structure did you choose to implement?

The chief purpose of my site is to provide the latest news about technological improvements in different sectors of computer science to its readers. The site supplies its users with info about sectors such as web development, artificial intelligence, game development, and app development. The target audience here are students and working professionals who want to stay updated on the go.

As the site is acting as an online news magazine for people in a fast-paced world, it is crucial that the user is able to get the most information they can in one glance. To achieve this goal, I have portrayed the information in the form of nicely organized tiles. This allows me to pack a lot of information in a small screen area while maintaining a look that pleases the eye. I have also tried to keep the design of the site as minimal as possible with nearly no use of any fancy colors (just a combination of white and grey). This helps in making the site look modern and gives it a unique aesthetic while being easy to look at. It also is perfect for people with color blindness. This added simplicity also makes the site easy to navigate which in turn helps in creating a good user experience.

The site begins with a navigation bar that has a very modern, minimal, and simple design. It features a logo in the beginning and then displays links guiding you to different pages containing content targeted to different fields in computer science. This can then adapt to different screen sizes so that users who are trying to watch content on a tablet or a phone are not ignored. As you may notice, the navigation bar is not a sticky one that follows you throughout the scrolling. This is done intentionally to reduce the clutter and make the site look minimal. This also provides the user a screen that has more content than having unnecessary litter filling the page.

Below the navigation bar comes the main content of the page. It starts with a big heading informing the user about the field of computer science the page targets. This helps make the user aware of what they are reading at all times. Below the main heading, you will find two sections of articles. To give them a slight separation and to make the site look a little cleaner, I have added a slight fading shade of grey as the background. Taking a look at the left section, you will be greeted with a big headline followed by smaller sub-headline articles. This makes the most important news stick out which helps guide the user to things that are the most important. With the world growing faster than ever before, advancements can be seen happening every second. To make the readers aware of the most recent activities in the industry in no time, I have added the latest article section showcasing the latest activities happening in the sector. This provides the user with the most recent news uplifting their knowledge whilst making them up to date with the industry. As I understand that knowing about the latest advancements in tech is as important as knowing about the biggest ones, I have added the latest article section on the right to the main articles. This way, the reader doesnâ€™t have to compromise on anything and has access to both the biggest news and the latest news without the need for any scrolling.

To let busy users get through their daily news without any bottlenecks, I have tried to use as little time-consuming animation as possible. The only animations that are evident are used to inform the user of any potential links they might have encountered. For eg, the zoom effect when hovering over images or quick text color changes when hovering over texts. This allows me to provide a site that has a decent level of aesthetics while not disrupting the user in any manner, making the experience pleasing and quick at the same time. When scrolling through the site, you may notice a small button with an arrow on the bottom right side of the page. This allows the user to get back on top without any additional scrolling making the process of navigating to the top swift and efficient. To free the user with a plethora of irritating pop-ups which have become a norm these days, I have tried to slim the number down to just one. The only pop-up I have is to inform the user of the weekly newsletter I provide and ask if they would like to subscribe. The pop-up will only be shown to the user the first time they visit the site which helps maintain the minimalism and ease of use the site is trying to achieve.

Scrolling to the bottom, you will find a footer that contains all the additional links and information about the page that you might be interested in. This includes pages that tell you more about the site as well as contact details. The footer also contains alternate links to the pages that talk about their own niche sub-topics of computer science. This will make locating pages way easier and will also be great for people who have already read everything on the current page.

On a closer inspection, you will find that every page has a similar layout. This helps in navigation for new users as a person who has been through one page feels like home when navigating through other pages. It also makes browsing through articles is a piece of cake for the avid readers of TPM.
